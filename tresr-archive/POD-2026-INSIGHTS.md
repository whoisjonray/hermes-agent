# POD 2026 Course Key Insights

Source: WeScale POD Course (instructors with $45M+ combined sales)
Reference Brand: Sloth Hiking Club - $800K year 1, $157K in 30 days

## Critical Learnings for TRESR Bot

### 1. BRANDED vs RANDOM
The course emphasizes building a **BRAND**, not just selling random designs.
- Customers buy for **community and belonging**, not just cool designs
- "They're not just buying for the shirt quality - they're buying for sense of community"
- This means: Group designs into cohesive brand collections, don't scatter

### 2. Niche Selection Framework
Before creating designs, validate the niche:
- Is the market large enough?
- Is there repeat purchase potential?
- Is the audience identifiable on social media?
- Are there sub-niches with passionate audiences?

**Example from course:**
- Started with "golf"
- Narrowed to "self-deprecating humor for bad/average golfers"
- Why? "90% of golfers claim they're not good" = massive relatable market

### 3. AI Tools They Use
- **Build My Pod** - Store setup automation
- **Brand Builder GPT** - Niche validation + brand naming
- **Design Generation** - (covered later in course)

### 4. Email is King
The email list is shown as primary revenue driver after initial acquisition.
- Repeat customers = real profit
- Build email from day 1

### 5. Design Philosophy
Looking for:
- **Relatable humor** - audience sees themselves
- **Community signals** - "only we get this" feeling
- **No IP risk** - generic references only
- **Simple execution** - 2-3 word brand names max

## Optimizations for TRESR Bot

### Current Gaps
1. We find random trends, not brand opportunities
2. No niche validation before design creation
3. Missing "customer journey" thinking
4. No cohesion between designs

### Recommended Changes
1. **Add niche scoring** - Before creating designs, score the niche
   - Market size (DataForSEO search volume)
   - Competition level
   - Repeat purchase potential
   - Social media identifiability

2. **Brand grouping** - Cluster related trends into brand opportunities
   - "3 coffee trends" = potential coffee brand
   - Not 3 random products

3. **Customer avatar** - For each niche, define WHO is buying
   - Demographics
   - Pain points
   - Where they hang out online

4. **Design series** - Create 3-5 related designs, not 1 random design
   - More cohesive
   - Better for ads (can test variations)
   - Builds brand feeling
